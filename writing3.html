Article for Project BachpanShala

Childhood doesn’t wait; neither should dreams.

Childhood, as fragile and fleeting as it is, is often taken for granted: celebrated in textbooks, recited in policy documents, yet rarely experienced in its full vibrancy by those who most need it.

BachpanShala, an initiative of the InAmigos Foundation, intervenes in this quiet tragedy, offering children lessons in living alongside lessons in reading and arithmetic. I am reminded, as I reflect upon this, of afternoons wandering through sunlit streets of my own youth; moments of curiosity and wonder that were never dictated by structure or compulsion, yet remain inaccessible to many.

At BachpanShala, children are nurtured through education that excites, healthcare that sustains, and safe spaces that encourage self-expression. Mentorship programs, skill-building workshops, and community engagement reignite curiosity, foster confidence, and allow dreams to germinate in soil previously barren. Each activity and every gesture becomes a quiet act of resistance against circumstances that seek to limit potential.

Supporting BachpanShala is not charity; it is a recognition that childhood, in its purest form, is a right, not a privilege. Volunteering, donating, or amplifying this cause allows us, as participants in this journey, to rewrite futures, one child at a time.

I find, in this work, a profound sense of gratitude and awe: gratitude for what we can give; awe at the resilience of children; and hope that life, despite its unpredictability, can flourish.
